What is DuckDuckGo?

DuckDuckGo is a search engine that is focused on privacy. It doesn’t save your search history to show you more relevant searches. The DuckDuckGo website states, “Over 96% of popular free Android apps we tested allow other companies to invade your privacy, like using your location to map everywhere you go.” duckduckgo.com

Strengths

Doesn’t Save Your Data

This is great for people who are paranoid about who knows where they are at all times. For example, if you search for ‘cars for sale,’ Google might get your location and then save it to use later for you. While DuckDuckGo would just ask for your location this one time then delete that data. This way no one could hack DuckDuckGo and then see where everyone lives.

Doesn’t Sell Your Information

Another great thing DDG (DuckDuckGo) gives you is peace of mind. This is because you know that people at Google, Facebook and other tech giants don’t have access to your data.

Loads Websites Faster

Websites would load faster because they aren’t running multiple processes to steal your data. When various processes aren’t taking all of your data, your computer can simply load the webpage to the browser window.

Weaknesses

Searches Aren’t Personalized

DDG doesn’t save your search history to train their AIs to display your desired content. Hence, you only get what you searched for as a result.

You will Still See Ads

While DDG does not sell your data, they still need to make money. So they show you ads based on your search only and not your past 200 searches.

Conclusion for DuckDuckGo

My rating for DDG is 9/10. The reason is that they don’t sell my data. Another thing I like about them is that they don’t display ads for my past searches. They simply show ads for my search.
